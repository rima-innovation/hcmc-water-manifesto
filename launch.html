HCMC Engineered Clean Water – Launch Document

Launch plan for an engineered clean‑water and water‑as‑a‑service platform in Ho Chi Minh City.
We replace unsafe tap water in HCMC with purified, transparently tested drinking water, delivered as a managed service to households, businesses, and institutions, with a premium hydrogen‑alkaline line for wellness‑focused customers.

1. Project Overview

We are launching an engineered clean‑water platform in Ho Chi Minh City that replaces unsafe tap water with purified, lab‑tested drinking water delivered through subscriptions and SLAs. The platform combines a core tap‑replacement line with a premium hydrogen‑alkaline wellness line, wrapped in transparent testing and plastic‑smart packaging.

  • Geography: Ho Chi Minh City (HCMC).
  • Model: recurring “water‑as‑a‑service” plus premium bottled products.
  • Plant: central treatment facility already built and to be commissioned.

2. Value Proposition and Products

2.1 Core Value Proposition

  • Safer than tap: multi‑stage treatment and batch‑level lab testing tuned to HCMC water risks.
  • Radically transparent: QR‑coded traceability and public test results per batch.
  • Always available: subscription delivery and stock management instead of ad‑hoc purchases.
  • Plastic‑smart: returnable and recycled packaging aligned with ESG expectations.

2.2 Product Lines

Line 1Everyday – Safe Tap Replacement

  • Ultra‑clean drinking water for daily use (drinking, cooking, coffee/tea, baby formula).
  • Delivered in returnable jugs for subscriptions and institutions; smaller retail formats where needed.

Line 2Performance – Hydrogen‑Alkaline Water

  • Hydrogen‑rich alkaline water with clearly specified pH and dissolved hydrogen concentration.
  • Targets gyms, clinics, offices, and higher‑income households.
  • Positioned as functional hydration (recovery, focus, vitality) without medical claims.

3. Target Market and Segments

3.1 Geography

  • Ho Chi Minh City, starting with districts where tap‑water trust and canal‑water quality are most problematic.
  • Initial focus on inner‑city districts and key growth corridors that support efficient routing.

3.2 Customer Segments

  • B2C households & apartments – middle‑income and above, apartment complexes, gated communities, villas.
  • B2B businesses – offices, co‑working spaces, SMEs, gyms, cafés, hotels.
  • Institutions – schools, training centers, clinics, private hospitals requiring higher assurance and reputation protection.

4. Service Model and Differentiation

4.1 Water‑as‑a‑Service (WaaS)

  • Subscriptions for households and SMEs (weekly / monthly delivery plans).
  • SLA‑backed contracts for institutions and larger corporates (delivery windows, minimum stock, response times).
  • Simple dashboards and reports for institutions showing water‑quality and delivery history per site.

4.2 Competitive Edge Pillars

  • Engineered, not just bottled: HCMC‑specific treatment and clear “tap vs. our water” comparisons.
  • HCMC‑localized transparency: QR batch traceability, public lab reports, no generic national label.
  • Premium hydrogen‑alkaline option: captures functional‑water and wellness demand.
  • Service‑led logistics: “never run out of safe water” promise and recurring relationships.
  • Plastic‑smart positioning: returnables and recycled content support ESG and regulatory trends.

5. Operations and Launch Phases

5.1 Technical & Regulatory Setup (Months 0–2)

  • Audit and optimise the plant to meet national drinking‑water standards and bottled‑water regulations.
  • Commission treatment lines, including hydrogen‑alkaline production and in‑house quality‑control lab.
  • Finalize HACCP and QA/QC protocols, including batch sampling and third‑party lab partnerships.

5.2 Pilot Launch (Months 3–5)

  • Select 1–2 pilot districts in HCMC with strong demand and manageable logistics.
  • Onboard:
    • 50–100 household subscriptions,
    • 10–20 B2B clients (offices, gyms, cafés),
    • 3–5 institutions (schools, clinics) as co‑pilot sites.
  • Validate unit economics, delivery reliability, customer satisfaction, and pricing for Everyday vs. Performance lines.

5.3 Scale‑Up (Months 6–12)

  • Expand coverage to additional districts, adding routes and capacity as needed.
  • Grow Performance line via partnerships (gyms, wellness, corporate wellness programs).
  • Win larger institutional contracts (private schools, hospitals, corporate campuses) with customised SLAs.

6. Marketing and Communication

6.1 Investor‑Facing Positioning

  • Engineered clean‑water platform in a Vietnam bottled‑water market growing at ~6–7%+ CAGR with premiumisation tailwinds.
  • Recurring, infrastructure‑like economics (subscriptions, SLAs, high switching costs).
  • Clear ESG and impact angle: water security, health, and plastic reduction in a major urban centre.

6.2 Consumer‑Facing Messaging

  • “Engineered clean water for HCMC — safer than your tap, delivered to your home, office, or school.”
  • Everyday line: reliable tap‑replacement water for families and kitchens.
  • Performance line: hydrogen‑alkaline wellness water for training, focus, and premium hydration.
  • Subscriptions and SLAs: “never run out of safe water.”
  • Plastic‑smart: returnables and recycled packaging that cut waste versus conventional bottled‑water habits.

7. Impact and ESG Narrative

  • Water security: increases access to safe, tested drinking water in HCMC, aligning with SDG‑6.
  • Health and wellness: safer everyday hydration plus evidence‑based functional‑water options.
  • Plastic and emissions: reduced single‑use plastic via returnables and local production; measurable, reportable impact metrics.
  • Strong fit for ESG / impact investors and potential PPP collaborations in Vietnam’s water sector.
